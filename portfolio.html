01 / About

The engineer
behind the work

I'm an aerospace engineering student at the University of Sheffield, with a strong focus on avionics, autonomous systems, and hands-on aircraft maintenance. I believe that engineering is only real when it flies — and I've had the privilege of working on real aircraft and building systems that actually get airborne.

During my internship at Asia Aerotechnic, I worked under licensed engineers on live commercial aircraft — replacing ELTs, rewiring GPS units, running ADS-B and TCAS verification tests, and performing bonding continuity checks for lightning protection.

At university, I led the avionics and mission planning for our VTOL quadplane group project — designing the autonomous flight mission end-to-end, tuning PID controllers, sizing batteries, and diagnosing faults on flight day to get the aircraft airborne.
